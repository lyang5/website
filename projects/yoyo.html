Role: Cambridge Skyline Design and Fabrication
Date: Fall 2014


With a team of 7, a fully functional yo-yo was manufactured. We decided to create a yoyo with the inside design being the Boston and Cambridge skyline. Instead of a static part, however, it would be freely rotating. The molds for each part were made by using a lathe and mill on aluminum blocks in accordance to our CAD and MasterCam specifications. The retaining ring and skylines were then injection molded while the clear dome cover was thermoformed. We were able to manufacture 50 yoyos.

Left: Cambridge Skyline. Right: Boston Skyline. Our design was first modeled in Solidworks and then the core and cavity molds for each part were made using MasterCam. Because of the small features on the Cambridge Skyline, the core mold design was waterjet on a plate first, then attached to an aluminum block.

The manufacturing process for one part. Multiple iterations were tested to find the optimal run time and color of the part.
